## 3. Copywriting Strategy & Market Differentiation
The copy structure applies specialized local restaurant conversion mechanics to differentiate a Dhaba from regular urban dining spots:
* **Sensory Utensil Integration:** Highlighting cooking apparatuses like *clay tandoors*, *iron kadhais*, and *earthen kulhads* triggers instant cultural trust and flavor associations that a digital consumer can practically smell through the screen.
* **The Highway Friction Buffer:** Roadside diners worry intensely about parking space, clean food, and delay. The copy strategically solves this by advertising "ample clean parking" and "rapid family seating" to drive casual drop-ins.
* **Visceral Vocabulary:** Replaces tired corporate adjectives like "excellent quality" or "premium dining" with grounding, appetizing terms like *smoky layers*, *piping hot*, *wood embers*, and *heavy swirl*.
